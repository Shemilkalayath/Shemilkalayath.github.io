Why History Matters for Economic Policy

Economists are often in a hurry to abstract away from history. The elegance of a formal model lies partly in its generality — its indifference to time and place. But the economies that economists study are not general. They are specific, particular, and shaped by their own pasts in ways that no model fully captures.

This is not a complaint against economic theory. It is an argument about its limits — and about what happens when policymakers mistake those limits for features.

The specific trajectories through which economies develop leave lasting marks on their institutional structures. You cannot understand where an economy is without understanding how it got there.
